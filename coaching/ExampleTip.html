Weekly Practice Building Tip
#37 The Right Marketing
 
 

Many brilliant diagnostic and treatment experts suffer financially because they don't understand this one thing: your practice is dependent on your ability to market.

Consider this metaphor: your practice is like a pond. Ponds need streams to bring in fresh water and release old water. This keeps the pond from turning stagnate and the fish alive. If your practice is your pond, then your fish are your patients, and your streams that keep everything alive are the marketing activities you do to bring new patients to the practice. As such, the goal of marketing is to design activities that give you exposure to patients who will stay, pay, and refer in order for your practice to succeed and prosper.

To be successful, each of your marketing streams must:

  • Express your intent. A successful marketing stream communicates clearly that your commitment is to find and serve people.
  • Build relationships with prospective patients. After all is said and done, a successful practice is the result of successful relationships.
  • Establish you as a person with moral authority. Moral Authority is the combination of your character and your ability to deliver the goods you promise.
  • Establish yourself as a bold, concise, and consistent repeater of truth. To achieve this objective you must embrace the power of honesty, which is the greatest practice building power you have.
  • Extend an invitation. To be a successful practice builder you must constantly find ways to extend an invitation to act. This can be difficult for doctors, but the fact of the matter is that people won't act without an invitation.
  • Be persistent. Don't be discouraged if your first attempts at marketing are not successful; you must stick with it. Keep doing the right things, for the right reasons, for a prolonged period of time, and you will succeed!

Truth: The success of your practice is dependent on your ability to market effectively.

Bottom line: Your ability to consistently market to patients who will stay, pay, and refer is the difference between a thriving practice/pond full of life and a stagnant one. Success is too important. You can't afford to let your practice/pond become stagnant.
